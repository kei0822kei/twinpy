Lattice¶

Inner Product and Metric Tensor¶

Given lattice matrix \(\boldsymbol{L}\) and two vectors \((x_1, y_1, z_1)^{T}\) and \((x_2, y_2, z_2)^{T}\), inner product can calculated as

\[[\boldsymbol{a}, \boldsymbol{b}, \boldsymbol{c}] = [\boldsymbol{e}_x, \boldsymbol{e}_y, \boldsymbol{e}_z] \boldsymbol{L}\]
\[\begin{split}( \boldsymbol{L} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ y_1 \\ z_1 \\ \end{pmatrix} )^{T} \boldsymbol{L} \begin{pmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \\ z_2 \\ \end{pmatrix} = (x_1, y_1, z_1) \boldsymbol{G} \begin{pmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \\ z_2 \\ \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

where metric tensor \(\boldsymbol{G}\)

\[\boldsymbol{G} = \boldsymbol{L}^{T} \boldsymbol{L}\]

Hexagonal Direction¶

Hexagonal Lattice¶

To create this object, hexagonal lattice \(\boldsymbol{H}\) and plane indices (three or four) are needed.

\[[\boldsymbol{a}_1, \boldsymbol{a}_2, \boldsymbol{c}] = [\boldsymbol{e}_x, \boldsymbol{e}_y, \boldsymbol{e}_z] \boldsymbol{H}\]

where

\[\begin{split}\boldsymbol{H} = \begin{pmatrix} a & -\frac{1}{2}a & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}a & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & c \\ \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

indices¶

Let input direction \(\boldsymbol{k}\) is \((UVW)\) in three indices and \((uvtw)\) in four indices. There are the relation between two kinds of direction indices as

\[\begin{split}\begin{pmatrix} U \\ V \\ W \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ w \\ \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

and

\[\begin{split}\begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ t \\ w \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} ( 2 U - V ) / 3 \\ ( 2 V - U ) / 3 \\ - ( u + v ) \\ W \\ \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

Hexagonal Plane¶

indices¶

Let input plane \(\boldsymbol{K}\) is \((HKL)\) in three indices and \((hkil)\) in four indices. There are the relation between two kinds of plane indices as

\[\begin{split}\begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} h \\ k \\ l \\ \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

and

\[\begin{split}\begin{pmatrix} h \\ k \\ i \\ l \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ -( H + K ) \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

direction normal to plane¶

When the vector normal to \(\boldsymbol{K}\) is \(\boldsymbol{k}\), then

\[\begin{split} [\boldsymbol{a}_1, \boldsymbol{a}_2, \boldsymbol{c}] \boldsymbol{k} = [\boldsymbol{a}^*_1, \boldsymbol{a}^*_2, \boldsymbol{c}^*] \begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix} = [\boldsymbol{e}_x, \boldsymbol{e}_y, \boldsymbol{e}_z] \boldsymbol{H}^* \begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix} = [\boldsymbol{a}_1, \boldsymbol{a}_2, \boldsymbol{c}] \boldsymbol{H}^{-1} \boldsymbol{H}^* \begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

so

\[\begin{split} \boldsymbol{k} = \boldsymbol{H}^{-1} \boldsymbol{H}^* \begin{pmatrix} H \\ K \\ L \\ \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

where \(\boldsymbol{H}^*\) is reciprocal lattice of hexagonal lattice \(\boldsymbol{H}\).

distance from plane¶

Let \(\boldsymbol{x}\) is the position in the basis of hexagonal lattice. Distance from the plane \(d\) are

\[d = |\boldsymbol{e}_{\boldsymbol{k}} \cdot \boldsymbol{H} \boldsymbol{x}|\]

where boldsymbol{e}_{boldsymbol{k}} is unit vector along \(\boldsymbol{k}\) direction on the basis of \([\boldsymbol{e}_x, \boldsymbol{e}_y, \boldsymbol{e}_z]\)

\[\boldsymbol{e}_{\boldsymbol{k}} = \frac{\boldsymbol{H}\boldsymbol{k}}{|\boldsymbol{H}\boldsymbol{k}|}\]
